Summary

Charlie Wilson's War is a political drama-comedy film directed by Mike Nichols, based on the true story of U.S. Congressman Charlie Wilson and his covert dealings in Afghanistan during the Soviet-Afghan War. The film stars Tom Hanks as Wilson, Julia Roberts as socialite Joanne Herring, and Philip Seymour Hoffman as CIA operative Gust Avrakotos. It explores themes of political maneuvering, the complexities of foreign intervention, and the unintended consequences of U.S. foreign policy. The narrative highlights Wilson's efforts to fund the Afghan mujahideen, showcasing both the humor and gravity of the situation. The film received critical acclaim for its sharp dialogue, strong performances, and insightful commentary on the political landscape of the time.
