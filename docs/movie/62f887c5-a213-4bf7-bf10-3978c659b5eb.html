Summary

The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy is a British science fiction comedy television series based on Douglas Adams' radio series of the same name. The show first aired in 1981 and follows the adventures of an unwitting human, Arthur Dent, who is swept off Earth just before its destruction. Accompanied by his alien friend Ford Prefect, Arthur navigates the galaxy, encountering a variety of eccentric characters and absurd situations. The series is known for its witty dialogue, satirical humor, and imaginative storytelling, exploring themes of existence, technology, and the universe. It has since gained a cult following and has been adapted into various other media, including books, films, and stage productions.
