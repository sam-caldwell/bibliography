Summary

This article provides some historical context for the ever-present battle between privacy interests and the government's interest in accessing information under the cover of "public safety." In this article the Clinton-era movement to install an NSA-designed "clipper chip" on every telecommunications device and its demise are discussed. This is especially relevant where it discusses the discovery of a security flaw by AT&T Bell Laboratories researchers late in the controversy, demonstrating the dangers of so-called "back doors" to encryption
