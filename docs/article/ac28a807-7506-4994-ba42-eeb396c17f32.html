Summary

This article provides a rarely discussed view of Zero Trust Network Architecture (ZTNA), which is often drowned out by marketing companies from ZTNA vendors like Zscaler, Palo Alto Networks or Microsoft. In this discussion, many concerns and counterpoints to the mainstream thought are made and possible solutions are suggested.
